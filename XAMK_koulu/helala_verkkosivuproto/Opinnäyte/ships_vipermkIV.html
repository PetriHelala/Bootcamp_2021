Description

Faulcon deLacy's Viper Mk. IV takes the Mk.III model and beefs it up to a heavy fighter role. While it lacks the speed of the older ship, it enjoys much better protection from its shields and armour.

With the capacity for a more capable powerplant and frame shift drive, the Mk.IV is also better suited for long-range patrols and rapid deployment. The powerplant upgrade potential supports greater customization for weapons and modules, making this a versatile fighter that would be and excellent addition to any navy or private-security concern.

The Viper Mk IV looks to be a suitable replacer for Cobra Mk III as an all-rounder trading and combat style ship.

Hardpoints include: 2 Class 1 Small hardpoints and 2 Class 2 Medium hardpoints.

Types of Ships

Multipurpose

Ships can be designed and retrofitted to suit multipurpose tasks, whether it be hauling cargo, ship-to-ship combat, or exploration. Multipurpose ships can do it all.

Combat

Typically faster and more agile, these types of ships can be retrofitted with advanced weaponry outrivaling other more all around ships. They serve their purpose in bounty hunting, pirating and escort missions, and are a must in any serious commanders arsenal.

Freighter

Slow, cumbersome and light in terms of armament, but extremely handy in their hauling capabilites. Used by traders and smugglers, these giants provide the space for goods no other ship can match.

Explorer

For the sightseeing fan in the galaxy. Large default jump ranges, fuel tanks large for their size. You can optimize jump distance through fitting lots of low class Internal Compartments for scanners. Ignoring the need to constantly fuel up and having to go close proximity with possible goods to detect them, these ships are designed with this in mind.

Passenger

For couriers, transporting both passengers and valuable items alike, these ships are a must have. Elegant and fast, designed to quickly and comfortably get from one system to the next. These ships have come to be known as space-limousines.
